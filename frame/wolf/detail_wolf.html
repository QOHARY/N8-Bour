Wolf-1061- c
Overview

Wolf 1061 c is a super Earth exoplanet that orbits an M-type star. Its mass is 3.41 Earths, it takes 17.9 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.089 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2015.

How It Was Discovered

UNSW Australia astronomers have discovered the closest potentially habitable planet found outside our solar system so far, orbiting a star just 14 light-years away. The planet, more than four times the mass of the Earth, is one of three that the team detected around a red dwarf star called Wolf 1061.
While a few other planets have been found that orbit stars closer to us than Wolf 1061, those planets are not considered to be remotely habitable.

Goldilocks Zone

Dr. Duncan Wright from UNSW, the lead author of the study, stated that the discovery is particularly exciting because all three planets are low enough in mass to potentially be rocky with solid surfaces, and the middle planet, Wolf 1061c, lies within the "Goldilocks" zone, where liquid water—and possibly even life—could exist.

Future Study

“The close proximity of the planets around Wolf 1061 means there is a good chance these planets may pass across the face of the star. If they do, then it may be possible to study the atmospheres of these planets in future to see whether they would be conducive to life,” says team member UNSW’s Dr Rob Wittenmyer.

Suitable For Life

Dr. Rob Wittenmyer from UNSW, a member of the team, noted that the close proximity of the planets around Wolf 1061 increases the likelihood that these planets may pass in front of the star, which could allow future studies of their atmospheres to determine if they might be suitable for life.

Rocky World

Professor Chris Tinney, head of the Exoplanetary Science group at UNSW, explained that their team has developed a new technique to improve the analysis of data from this precise, purpose-built, planet-hunting instrument and has studied over a decade's worth of observations of Wolf 1061. He added that these planets, located right next to us, join the small but growing group of potentially habitable rocky worlds orbiting stars cooler than our Sun.
